Inspiration

While playing PUBG (PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds), I got really drawn to how its real-time multiplayer systems worked. It made me curious about what it would take for a solo developer to build something along those lines. I also see a growing demand for online games that bring people together, adding a layer of social interaction beyond just the gameplay.

Overview

This project is my ongoing journey of building a first-person shooter from the ground up, exploring both gameplay design and multiplayer networking. The development is divided into multiple phases, each targeting a key aspect of modern FPS games:

Walkthrough

User Registration System

Account information entered by users will be recorded and stored on the server for persistent data management.

Combat Arena & Weapon Spawning

Entering the combat scene where weapons are randomly generated on the battlefield. Players can pick up weapons like electric shock weapons by interacting with them.

Player Controls & Weapon System

Control movement with keyboard (forward/backward) or on-screen joystick. Mouse controls camera rotation and vertical aiming. Use the fire button on the right side to shoot, with each weapon having limited ammunition.

AI Combat & Data Updates

Use crosshair to aim at patrolling AI enemies. When enemies are defeated, data updates automatically and the enemy count on the battlefield decreases.
